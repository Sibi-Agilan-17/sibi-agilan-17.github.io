About Our Project

“Climate Change and Its Impact on Agriculture, Soils, and Animal Husbandry” examines how rising temperatures and erratic weather patterns affect farming systems, soils, and livestock — and what communities can do to adapt.

  • Crop yields: altered growing seasons, heat stress and yield variability.
  • Soil health: increased erosion, loss of organic matter, and changing nutrient cycles.
  • Livestock well‑being: heat-related stress, disease vulnerability, and productivity impacts.

The delegation focuses on developing practical, community-driven strategies to build resilience in local farming systems under the 1.5°C Challenge and SDG 2: Zero Hunger.

Our 3-D Solution: Develop, Drive, Digitise

  • Develop — Strengthen soil fertility and farm productivity through regenerative and sustainable practices.

  • Drive — Empower youth and farmers through workshops, outreach, and field demonstrations on climate-smart methods.

  • Digitise — Use data and digital tools to monitor soil health, track climate impacts, and share knowledge widely.

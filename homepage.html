Sir Sivaswami Kalalaya — Delegation at the 9th Active Impact Summit

Representing India at the 1M1B Active Impact Summit 2025, the Sir Sivaswami Kalalaya delegation contributes to the 1.5°C Challenge under SDG 2: Zero Hunger, focusing on climate-resilient agriculture, soil health, and sustainable animal husbandry.

Our project, “Climate Change and Its Impact on Agriculture, Soils, and Animal Husbandry,” explores how temperature rise and erratic weather affect crop yield, livestock health, and soil ecosystems — and how innovation and community awareness can build resilience at the local level.

About Our Project

“Climate Change and Its Impact on Agriculture, Soils, and Animal Husbandry” explores how rising temperatures and erratic weather patterns affect crop yields, soil health, and livestock well-being. The delegation focuses on developing practical, community-driven strategies to build resilience in local farming systems under the 1.5°C Challenge and SDG 2: Zero Hunger.

Our 3-D Solution: Develop, Drive, Digitise

  • Develop — Strengthen soil fertility and farm productivity through regenerative and sustainable practices.
  • Drive — Empower youth and farmers through workshops, outreach, and field demonstrations on climate-smart methods.
  • Digitise — Use data and digital tools to monitor soil health, track climate impacts, and share knowledge widely.
